# High-risk drug monitoring (illustrative demo corpus)

Paraphrased from public UK primary-care monitoring guidance (BNF / NICE CKS style).
Illustrative and simplified for a demo — not a substitute for current guidance.

## Methotrexate
Patients on methotrexate need full blood count (FBC), urea and electrolytes (U&E) and liver
function tests (LFT) monitored regularly — typically every 12 weeks once stable, and more often
after starting or a dose change. Stop and seek advice if there are signs of bone-marrow
suppression, liver toxicity or breathlessness.

## Lithium
Lithium requires a serum lithium level every 3 months once stable, taken about 12 hours after the
dose. Renal function (U&E) and thyroid function (TFT) should be checked every 6 months. Check levels
more frequently after a dose change or if the patient is unwell or dehydrated.

## Amiodarone
Amiodarone needs thyroid function tests (TFT) and liver function tests (LFT) every 6 months, because
it can cause both hypo- and hyperthyroidism and hepatotoxicity.

## Azathioprine
Azathioprine requires FBC and LFT monitoring, typically every 12 weeks once stable, with more
frequent checks after initiation or dose change.

## ACE inhibitors and ARBs
For ACE inhibitors (e.g. ramipril, lisinopril) and ARBs (e.g. losartan), check renal function and
electrolytes (U&E) within 1–2 weeks of starting or increasing the dose, then at least annually once
stable. A small rise in creatinine can be acceptable; a large rise needs review.

## Diuretics
For diuretics such as spironolactone, monitor U&E — checking after starting/changing the dose and at
least annually, watching potassium in particular.

## Warfarin
Warfarin dosing is guided by the INR. The testing interval varies with stability; a patient with no
INR recorded in the last 12 weeks should be flagged for review.
