# Long-term condition monitoring (illustrative demo corpus)

Paraphrased from public UK primary-care guidance (NICE / QOF style). Illustrative and simplified —
not a substitute for current guidance.

## Type 2 diabetes
Patients on the diabetes register should have HbA1c checked roughly every 3–6 months until stable,
then at least every 6 months. An annual review should include foot examination, retinal screening,
blood pressure, renal function and a review of cardiovascular risk.

## Chronic kidney disease (CKD)
CKD monitoring includes renal function (U&E / eGFR) and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (ACR) at
least annually; the frequency increases with more advanced or progressing disease.

## Hypertension
Patients with hypertension should have blood pressure reviewed at least annually, along with
assessment of cardiovascular risk and, where relevant, renal function and electrolytes.

## COPD
COPD patients should have at least an annual review including symptoms, inhaler technique, smoking
status, exacerbation history and spirometry where appropriate.

## Asthma
Asthma patients should have at least an annual asthma review covering control, inhaler technique,
adherence and a personalised action plan; review sooner after an exacerbation.
